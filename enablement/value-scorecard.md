# Value Scorecard

Tying the agent to P&L: avoided-cost framing with honest counterfactual structure.

## Value Framework

The agent creates value through **early warning** -- providing actionable lead time
before grid stress events that would otherwise be detected later or not at all.

### Avoided-Cost Categories

| Category | Mechanism | Measurability |
|----------|-----------|---------------|
| Load-shed avoided | Early DR activation prevents involuntary curtailment | High (MW x duration x VOLL) |
| Imbalance cost avoided | Earlier reserve positioning reduces real-time price exposure | High (settlement data) |
| Maintenance deferral | Canceling planned outages before stress events | Medium (counterfactual) |
| Regulatory penalty avoided | Demonstrating reliability compliance | Low (probabilistic) |

### Counterfactual Structure

Every value claim carries a visible counterfactual assumption:

> "If the agent's Tier 4 alert at T-6h had triggered DR activation,
> and if DR had reduced peak demand by X MW,
> then Y MW of firm load shed would have been avoided,
> valued at $Z using VOLL of $V/MWh."

Each "if" is an assumption. Each assumption is printed on the figure (F12).

### What We Do NOT Claim

- We do not claim the agent **would have prevented** Uri or Elliott
- We do not claim specific dollar savings without visible assumptions
- We do not attribute value without a counterfactual baseline
- We do not extrapolate backtest results to future performance

*The honesty IS the differentiation.*
